Court Opinion Elements

Case Background

New York Times Co. v. Sullivan, 376 U.S. 254(1964) is a landmark case of the United States Supreme Court that established the "actual malice" standard, greatly protecting freedom of the press.

Key Elements of the Court Opinion

  • Case Name and Citation：New York Times Co. v. Sullivan, 376 U.S. 254 (1964)
  • Petitioner: New York Times Company
  • Respondent: L.B. Sullivan, Montgomery City Commissioner
  • Appellate Court: Supreme Court of the United States
  • Date of Judgment：March 9, 1964
  • Chief Justice: William J. Brennan, Jr
  • Case Facts：
    • The New York Times published a civil rights advertisement criticizing officials in Montgomery, Alabama.
    • Police Chief L.B. Sullivan believed the ad was directed at him and filed a defamation lawsuit.
    • The Alabama court ruled that The New York Times must pay Sullivan $500,000.
  • Legal Issues：
    • Does the First Amendment limit public officials from filing defamation lawsuits concerning statements about their official actions?
    • Do state defamation laws violate the First and Fourteenth Amendments' guarantees of freedom of speech and press?
  • Holding：
    • It unanimously overturned the Alabama court's ruling.
    • It established the 'actual malice' standard: public officials must prove that the defendant knew the statement was false or acted with reckless disregard for the truth in order to win a defamation lawsuit.
  • Legal reasoning：
    • The First Amendment protects criticism of public officials' official actions, even if it contains factual errors.
    • Lack of the 'actual malice' standard can lead to media self-censorship, suppressing public debate.
    • Criticism of public officials should have 'breathing space' to ensure public discussion.
  • Significance：
    • Significantly strengthened the protection of press freedom in the United States.
    • Later extended to public figures.
    • It is becoming an important reference for the protection of freedom of speech worldwide.

典型句型

The Court held that the First Amendment protects the publication of all statements, even false ones, about the conduct of public officials except when made with actual malice.
法院认为，第一修正案保护所有关于公职人员行为的陈述，即使是虚假陈述，除非是出于实际恶意。
A public official cannot recover damages for a defamatory falsehood relating to his official conduct unless he proves that the statement was made with actual malice.
公职人员不能就其官方行为的诽谤性虚假陈述获得损害赔偿，除非他证明该陈述是出于实际恶意。
The actual malice standard requires that the defendant knew the statement was false or acted with reckless disregard of whether it was false or not.
实际恶意标准要求被告明知陈述虚假或罔顾其真假。
This case established an important precedent for press freedom in the United States.
这个案件为美国的新闻自由确立了一个重要先例。
The ruling significantly expanded the protections afforded to the press under the First Amendment.
这一裁决显著扩大了第一修正案对新闻媒体的保护。
